AN ASIDE ON SKILL CHECKS

We have identified the traditional skill check as being ass  
This is for the following reasons:

* Binary pass/fail allows for little player agency  
* Usually the negative consequences of the skill check are either who cares or it’s a save-or-die  
* This doesn’t drive play

So instead we will do this:

* Skill checks should only be done as part of scenarios  
* Every passed or failed skill check leads to the next decision gate  
* If hard-pressed players can auto-pass a roll. This gives them a negative karma “shitcoin” that the DM can use to fuck them over in the future. Such shitcoins can also be gained by generating bad karma in game; good karma awards wincoins that you can use to do this for “free”

### Ranged Weapon Keywords

**Heavy:**  
Heavy weapons must be **braced** before they can be fired. Firing a heavy weapon without bracing is considered **hip-firing**.

**Rapid Fire:**  
These weapons unleash a hail of fire that is nigh inescapable at close ranges. When you attack with a rapid fire weapon and your target is half the weapon’s range or less away from you, you make each attack roll with \+2 automatic successes.

**Assault:**  
These weapons are designed to be used in rapid advances, where long aim times and high-quality stabilization are luxuries not available to the wielder. When an **Assault** weapon is hip-fired, the penalty to hit is halved (-1 instead of \-2 for single fire, \-2 instead of \-4 for automatic fire).

**Sidearm:**  
These weapons are very quick to draw. As long as you have the requisite number of hands available, you can draw and stow a sidearm once per turn as a free action. If you are wielding a two-handed weapon, you are considered to have a hand free for the purpose of drawing a one-handed sidearm.

## Combat Mechanics

### Actions

In combat (or other structured game time), everytime you wish to do something, you must take an action to do it. Actions consume action points \-- every character has 4 of them they can use on a single turn. Actions may consume between 0 and 3 action points. An action consuming 0 AP is called a Free Action, one consuming 1 AP is called a Minor Action, 2 AP are Standard Actions and 3 AP are Full Actions. The different actions available to every character and their action point consumptions are detailed below.

## Explanation of Actions

### Special Actions

#### Move

When you take a move action, you can move up to your speed. Move actions cost progressively more action points as you take them; the first one costs 1, the second 2 and so on.

#### Use Item

You can use an action to make use of an item in your possession. The amount of action points required for this varies depending on the item; it is usually a standard action.

#### Use Power

You can use a combat power or magic spell available to you, consuming action points as specified in the power’s description. Most powers can be used as a standard action, but some may also require a full action.

#### Use Skill

On occasion, you may wish to take an action in combat that requires a skill roll (such as using Acrobatics to run over a cable, using Mechanics to restart a stuck mechanism, etc.) The GM adjudicates the number of action points using a skill requires depending on the situation.

### Minor Actions

Minor actions cost 1 AP each to perform.

#### Brace

You can brace a heavy weapon to stabilize it. This can mean propping it against a stable surface, or taking a more stable position to fire it from. Once you have braced a heavy weapon, you remain braced until you move it from the braced position. While you have your heavy weapon braced, you can fire it normally 

#### Disengage

When you are in melee combat, you can disengage to move one square in any direction of your choosing. This movement does not provoke attacks of opportunity.

#### Draw/Stash

As a minor action, you can draw a weapon or item you have equipped to wield it, or you can 

#### Drop Prone

You can drop prone as a minor action. Doing so increases your evasion by 1 point. While you are prone, you can only crawl to move, reducing your land movement speed to half. 

#### Take Aim

You can take aim at a target to increase your accuracy against it. When you do so, you can make your next ranged attack against the target with \+2 automatic successes.

### Standard Action

#### Hipfire

If you are wielding a ranged weapon capable of automatic fire, you can choose to fire it from the hip. When you fire your weapon from the hip, you can make attacks up to its rate of fire, but you make every attack roll at \-3 successes.

#### Stand Up

If you are prone, you can stand up as a standard action.

#### Standard Attack

You make a single attack roll against the target with a weapon you are wielding.

### Full Action

Full actions cost 3 AP to perform.

#### Automatic Fire

If you are wielding a ranged weapon capable of automatic fire, you can take the multiattack action to make attacks up to your weapon’s rate of fire.

#### Charge

To take a charge, declare a target you can see. You must move toward that target in a straight line, moving up to twice your speed and ending your charge directly adjacent to your target. At the end of the charge, you can make a standard melee attack with a weapon you are wielding, or use a power that replaces your melee attack in a charge. You make any attack rolls in a charge with \+2 automatic successes.

#### Multiattack

If you are wielding a melee weapon capable of making multiple attacks, you can take the multiattack action to make attacks up to your weapon’s attack rate.

### Reactions

Reactions are special actions that you can take when it is not your turn. In general, reactions do not consume AP, but you can only take one of them per round. Every character automatically has access to the following reactions.

#### Counterspell

When another creature within 20 squares uses a magic power of a type that you can currently cast (for example, an Arcane spell while you are wielding an Arcane catalyst), you can counterspell as a reaction. To counterspell, roll the same check as the one used to cast the spell; if you score more successes than the original caster, their spell fizzles without effect.

#### Dive

When you would be damaged by an area attack or magical effect, you can roll your Dodge characteristic against the difficulty of the attack. If you beat the difficulty, you tumble out of the attack’s range and are unaffected by it.

#### Fire Overwatch

If you are wielding a ranged weapon and are targeted for a charge, you can fire (automatically if the weapon is capable of it) at the charging enemy. This is always considered hip-fire.

#### Opportunity Attack

When you are wielding a melee weapon, and another creature takes a move action that passes through your weapon reach and does not end next to you, you can immediately make a weapon attack against it.

The XP necessary to level up increases slowly; this means you must buy more or more expensive advances as time goes on to be able to level up.

### Campaign Advancement (Elite Advances)

The final type of character progression is Elite Advances. An Elite Advance represents something more total than individual battle talents, powers, or skills. It represents a fundamental facet of the character. Perhaps this is an element of their shrouded past, a divine favor, an innate talent of such magnitude it is not covered by the existing systems. Elite Advances cannot be bought with points, and cannot truly be pursued; they are unlocked by your actions during the campaign. Every Elite Advance has its own criteria for unlocking. Due to the nature of Elite Advances, the GM should be very circumspect in approaching them. As a rule of thumb, a character should gain one or two per tier, and usually end the game (if played for the full 30 levels) with four or five.

## Miscellaneous Rule Terms

KEYWORD: Warp  
This ability uses the Warp (pending copyright claims) to function and can be blocked by barriers in that terrain.

RULE: SUMMONING  
When you cast a spell that summons or creates a magical creature, you can choose to control that creature. While the creature is under your control, you and the summoned creature share an AP pool. You can control only one creature at a time (any abilities you might unlock notwithstanding). A summoned creature has its own set of actions, skills and powers, but its equipment partially depends on the summoner. In particular, for the purposes of calculating damage, a summoned creature uses the increment of the catalyst used to summon it.

RULE: ARCANE SPELLS  
Arcane spells, once learned, can be equipped in any spell slot of rank at least the minimum rank of the spell. Some spells can become more powerful when cast at higher ranks, gaining a level for every number of ranks above the minimum ranks they are cast at. The spells effects usually depend on its casting level. For example, Lightning Bolt I (a Rank 1 spell) does one increment of damage to one target, at a cost of one increment of focus. Lightning Bolt VI, the same spell cast at Rank 6, does one increment of damage to up to six targets, cast at a cost of six increments of focus.

### Strain and Fatigue

Even the immortal Deathless, while their energy exceeds that of ordinary mortals greatly, must contend with exhaustion. This is represented in the game by **strain**. Every character has a strain value, measured from 0 to 20\.

#### Effects of Strain

While your strain ranges from 0 to 10, it has no deleterious effects on you. When it begins to exceed this value, the accrued damage to your essence begins to hinder you. Starting at 11 strain, you always discorporate when you die, regardless of the severity of your wounds.  
Starting at 15 strain, you lose your ability to use your Hyper Regeneration. (Potions and magic that inflict strain in exchange for recovery still work.) All healing you receive is only half as effective.  
When you reach 20 strain, you reach your breaking point. If you are dead and your strain is 20, you cannot reincorporate for a month. Whenever you take damage at 20 strain, you must take a Grit test (Difficulty 9\) or discorporate immediately. If you would gain further strain for any reason while at 20 strain, you discorporate immediately. 

#### Sources of Strain

Strain represents the aggregate effects of damage and exhaustion upon you. You gain strain the following ways:  
**Death.** If you die, you automatically gain 3 strain. Certain methods of revival may inflict additional strain.  
**Hyper Regeneration.** Ordinarily, you recover 25% of your health and half of your focus in 1 hour. If you use your Hyper Regeneration to speed up this process, you gain strain accordingly.  
**Use of certain items.** Some consumables cause you to gain strain in exchange for their beneficial effects.  
**Fatigue.** When you perform long stretches of strenuous activity without rest, you can gain strain from the exhaustion. See the section on Fatigue for more details.

#### Recovering from Strain

Unlike physical wounds, strain to some extent is a measure of damage to your immortal essence. This means that recovery from it can be laborious. Under normal conditions, a character who recuperates for a week loses 5 strain. Certain blessed, magically powerful locations can also enable you to recover from strain. When you take a short rest on such blessed ground, you lose 5 strain as if you had recuperated for a week. You can only gain this benefit of each blessed ground once a month.
