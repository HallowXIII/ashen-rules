# The Mad Alchemist - Ideation Document

The Mad Alchemist is a Tier 1 adventure for the Ashen role-playing game system.
It is intended to teach new players and GMs the ropes of the system and illustrate
unconventional features to inspire new players.

## Goals

The adventure should:
- be addressed to a first-time GM
- provide an opportunity to explore all subsystems of the gameplay loop
- provide a base to let the players continue with their own adventures after it ends

## Basic Idea

- Assumption: the adventure is the first adventure run for a group
- Situation: the adventure plays in a location on the outskirts of Venna Weald clear,
  near the settlement of Keshto Village. Mysterious goings on have been 
  reported here recently. After the death of several peacekeepers and civilians under
  suspicious circumstances, the leadership of the Never-Still has decided to put out
  a contract under the auspices of the Black Guard for interested Deathless to
  investigate the situation. 
  => Hook for players: why is their character here? Are they Never-Still and sent to
     deal with the problem or at least supervise? Are they Free Agents in search of 
     coin? Are they with another faction sent here for reasons that are beyond them?
- Overarching structure of the adventure: 
  -> multiple locations that can be visited in (nearly) any order -- the final mystery
     requires having completed all subobjectives to solve, but its location can be
     explored beforehand
  -> the plot thickens with every set of clues
  -> the final encounter can drop a hint for the next adventure the GM wants to run

## The Villain: Šauhahten, "The Mad Alchemist"

### Identity
- **Race:** Mirror (Echo) — a child of Might-Be, the Queen of Summer
- **Former faction:** The Most Ancient and Noble Order of Mortefactors
- **Name meaning:** "the knowledgeable, the wise" — self-chosen or sarcastically 
  bestowed; either way, deeply ironic given his situation

### Background
Šauhahten was once a Mortefactor researcher with access to the faction's most
sensitive work — their investigation into Prima Materia, conducted as part of
efforts to understand and ultimately free their imprisoned leader, Grandfather
Bones. He was expelled from the order when he began advocating for (or possibly
already conducting) experiments on souls, crossing ethical lines the faction
would not tolerate.

The irony of his origin runs deep: as a Mirror, his people's natural allegiance
lies with the Never-Still and the restoration of the Faerie Realm. Instead, he
abandoned that cause to study death with the Mortefactors, was expelled for
going too far, and now takes guidance from a voice in the very Fog that
destroyed his people's homeland.

### The Work
Šauhahten is attempting to create Prima Materia — the "forbidden" pinnacle of
alchemy, which requires the rendering down of souls. The historical precedent
is Vay Ngien, who used Deathless souls (presumably for their closer link to
divine power). Šauhahten is attempting the process with mortal souls instead.
His stated goal is to restart the broken cycle of death and rebirth. He
genuinely believes this is the path to healing the world.

### The Patron
After his expulsion, operating alone near the edges of Venna Weald, Šauhahten
encountered *something* in the Fog — a voice, a presence, an intelligence that
offered guidance, encouragement, and knowledge. He believes he has found a
collaborator. In reality, whatever he is communing with benefits from souls
being rendered down — it feeds on the process, or is being slowly reconstituted
by it. Šauhahten does not fully understand what he is talking to.

The adventure deliberately leaves the patron's nature undefined. A GM who wants
it to be a dead god's echo, a Faerie parasite, a Fog-born intelligence, or
something else entirely can slot in their own answer. The adventure only
establishes that something is there and that it is not what the alchemist thinks
it is.

### Personality
Šauhahten is calm, articulate, and utterly certain. He is not raving, not
cackling. He explains patiently why the murders were necessary and is confused
that you don't agree. The "mad" in "Mad Alchemist" is what the locals call him
— face to face, he is the most reasonable person in the room, and that is what
makes him frightening.

When the party threatens his work directly — smashing equipment, burning notes,
destroying his laboratory — the mask cracks. Out comes the wounded grandiose
narcissism of an immortal fey being who has spent decades convincing himself
he is the only one who truly understands the world's suffering.

### Role in the Adventure
- The party should encounter evidence of Šauhahten (journals, effects of his
  work, NPC testimony) well before meeting him in person.
- Ideally, the party has a chance to speak with him, hear his reasoning, and
  perhaps be tempted to agree before deciding to stop him.
- The real victory condition is destroying his laboratory, research, and
  stockpiled materials — setting him back years or decades.
- Šauhahten cannot be permanently killed (he is Deathless). The party wins the
  adventure but cannot end the threat. He will reincorporate and may become a
  recurring antagonist. This serves double duty: it is a dramatic moment and a
  system lesson (Deathless means Deathless).

## Adventure Structure

### What the Party Needs to Discover

The investigation has four threads, each building toward the finale:
1. **The facts:** people are disappearing (not confirmed dead), the circumstances
   are strange — what's actually happening?
2. **The what:** something is being done to souls specifically — this is not a 
   mundane killer or a predatory creature.
3. **The who:** a Deathless ex-Mortefactor Mirror operating in the area.
4. **The where:** the laboratory's location, hidden beneath an old Faerie site
   the party may have already visited.

### Phase 1: Investigation in Keshto Village (Social / Skills)

The party arrives and gathers information. This phase teaches social interaction
and skill checks.

- **The disappearances:** a mix of peacekeepers and civilians have gone missing.
  Nobody taken from inside the settlement — all were alone when they vanished
  (foragers, patrols, travelers between settlements). The disappearances cluster
  in a particular direction from the settlement, along routes skirting the edge
  of the Clear where the Fog thickens. The timeline shows they are accelerating.
- **What's been tried:** the Never-Still sent peacekeepers to investigate; some
  of those went missing too. That's when they escalated to a Black Guard
  contract.
- **A surviving peacekeeper:** one peacekeeper made it back. He didn't see
  anything directly, but can point the party to the area where his colleague
  disappeared. This is the bridge from settlement investigation to field
  exploration.
- **Early Faerie site visit:** Merzaghaal may send the party to check a nearby
  old Faerie site as a routine task ("strange things are happening, go see if
  the old shrine has been disturbed"). The surface level is exactly what it
  appears to be — ancient, overgrown, resonant with faded magic. The party
  finds nothing suspicious unless they already know what to look for. The
  laboratory is hidden beneath it.

### Phase 2: Field Exploration (Exploration / Investigation)

The party follows leads into the wilderness outside the Clear. This phase
teaches exploration, hazards, and environmental skills. The two field locations
can be found in either order; finding one makes finding the other easier, as
the bodies are transported between them.

#### The Dump Site
Where Šauhahten disposes of the bodies after extracting their souls.

- **What you find:** corpses that are *wrong* — an Occultism or Medicine check
  reveals something has been done beyond simple murder. They are husks.
  Whatever animating force a living person has, it's been extracted.
- **What it tells you (alone):** souls have been extracted, victims were brought
  here post-mortem, someone is being methodical about disposal. You can
  backtrack the trail to the ritual site.
- **System lesson:** investigation skills, reading a scene, understanding that
  the threat is supernatural.

#### The Ritual Site
Where Šauhahten performs the soul extractions. A mundane, grim location — a
cave, an abandoned outbuilding, something unglamorous. The ugly work happens
in an ugly place; the refined alchemy happens elsewhere.

- **What you find:** evidence of repeated use — scoring on stone, remnants of
  ritual circles, alchemical apparatus too heavy or fragile to move. The
  Fog behaves strangely here. Alchemical residue.
- **What it tells you (alone):** this is where the extractions happen, the
  method is alchemical and scholarly (not bestial or divine), someone has
  been using this place repeatedly. You can follow the disposal trail to the
  dump site.
- **What both together tell you:** the full scope of the operation, the volume
  of victims, the sophistication of the method — and critically, that neither
  location is where the alchemist *lives*. He comes, works, and leaves. The
  trail from the ritual site leads in a third direction, but goes cold (Fog,
  deliberate obscuring). Something at the ritual site identifies the technique
  as Mortefactor-derived — this sends the party back to Keshto Village.
- **System lesson:** exploration in hazardous terrain, Fog exposure,
  environmental awareness.

### Phase 3: The Mortefactor Connection (Social / Faction)

The party returns to Keshto Village with specific questions. This is a different
kind of social encounter — they are pulling on a faction thread, talking to
people who may have reasons to be evasive.

- **Merzaghaal** may know which unaffiliated Deathless have been operating in
  the area. With the party's new evidence, the pieces click.
- **Local Mortefactors** (if any are present) recognize the ritual techniques
  and react with alarm — this is their research being misused. They may
  volunteer Šauhahten's name and expulsion, or need persuading to air the
  faction's dirty laundry.
- **Ordinary residents** might remember a strange, unforgettable Mirror who
  lived on the outskirts. Polite, kept to himself, asked unusual questions,
  bought odd supplies.
- **Key information gained:** a name, a description, the fact that he's a
  Mirror (which connects to the Faerie site), and enough to determine the
  laboratory's location — either from supply routes, Mortefactor knowledge
  of what Prima Materia research requires (Fog proximity, ambient magical
  energy — exactly what an old Faerie site provides), or both.

### Phase 4: The Laboratory (Combat / Climax)

The party returns to the Faerie site, now understanding what is hidden beneath
it. The laboratory has layered defenses, each reflecting a different facet of
Šauhahten's identity:

#### Layer 1: Getting In
The hidden entrance requires specific knowledge to find and open — fey-derived
knowledge, an alchemical key, or a Faerie resonance cipher. This information
comes from the Mortefactors ("he stole a cipher when he left"), Šauhahten's own
notes at the ritual site, or careful re-examination of the shrine with the right
context. The party cannot brute-force their way in.

#### Layer 2: The Undead (Mortefactor Heritage)
The outer defense — expendable perimeter guards. Šauhahten knows how to bind
and raise undead, and uses them as sentries. The dark implication: these may be
reanimated husks of his victims, soulless bodies repurposed as guards. Players
who found the dump site earlier will recognize what they're fighting.

- **System lesson:** combat fundamentals, fighting undead.

#### Layer 3: Fey Illusions and Barriers (Mirror Birthright)
The middle layer. The Faerie ruin responds to Šauhahten in ways it wouldn't for
anyone else — false passages, rooms that loop, doors that aren't there, walls
that are. Brute force won't help; the party needs the information they gathered
(the cipher, the notes, the Mortefactor intel) to see through or bypass the
deceptions.

- **System lesson:** preparation and information have mechanical payoff; not
  every obstacle is solved by fighting.

#### Layer 4: The Alchemical Monstrosity (The Work Itself)
The innermost defense — something Šauhahten created, probably a byproduct or
proof-of-concept of his Prima Materia research. It should be visibly *wrong*
in a way that signals danger: too large, too fast, not behaving like a natural
creature. This thing is beyond a Tier 1 party's ability to fight head-on.

- **Intended solutions:** lure it into a trap, seal it behind a barrier, use the
  ruin's architecture against it, or simply run past it.
- **System lesson:** not every encounter is a fight; read the threat level and
  adapt.

#### Layer 5: Šauhahten
The alchemist himself. How much warning he has depends on how the party handled
the preceding layers — if they fought through the undead loudly and took too
long on the illusions, he is already destroying notes when they arrive. If they
were clever and quick, they catch him mid-work.

- Ideally there is a conversation before combat. He is calm, explains his
  reasoning, may offer the party a chance to walk away.
- The victory condition is destroying the laboratory — equipment, research,
  stockpiled materials.
- Šauhahten will fight to defend his work, escalating from composed certainty
  to grandiose narcissistic rage as his laboratory is destroyed around him.
- He cannot be permanently killed. The party may defeat him in combat, but he
  will reincorporate at his soul anchor in days. The adventure ends with a
  victory and an open threat.

## NPCs

### Merzaghaal (Quest-Giver)
- **Race:** Goldenbristle (Sootbeast)
- **Faction:** Never-Still (mid-level agent)
- **Role:** The party's principal point of contact. He issued the Black Guard
  contract on behalf of the Never-Still leadership.
- **Perspective:** Merzaghaal cares about this as a faction matter — Never-Still
  peacekeepers are among the missing, and there's a potential threat near an
  old Faerie site that the faction considers significant. He's competent and
  professional, but his priorities are the faction's priorities.
- **What he provides:** the job briefing, context on the Faerie site (sends the
  party there early as a routine check), faction resources, and a sounding
  board when the party returns with new information. He may recognize
  Šauhahten's name or description once the party has it, or know whom to ask.

### The Town Reeve
- **Race:** TBD (likely mortal — human or Fogborn)
- **Role:** The local authority responsible for day-to-day peacekeeping in the
  settlement. The reeve is the one fielding panicked families, organizing
  search parties, and dealing with the human cost of the disappearances.
- **Perspective:** The reeve cares because their *people* are missing — farmers,
  foragers, ordinary folk. There may be tension with Merzaghaal: it took dead
  peacekeepers to get faction attention, and the reeve may resent this or
  simply be grateful the contract was finally issued. Either way, the reeve
  offers a different lens on the same problem.
- **What they provide:** the facts of the disappearances — who's missing, when
  they vanished, where they were last seen, which areas have been searched.
  The reeve introduces the party to the surviving peacekeeper and hands over
  whatever records exist. They are the party's primary source in Phase 1.

### The Surviving Peacekeeper
- **Race:** TBD
- **Role:** The bridge from the settlement investigation (Phase 1) to field
  exploration (Phase 2). One of the peacekeepers sent to investigate the
  disappearances who made it back.
- **Perspective:** Shaken, possibly guilty about surviving when his colleague
  didn't. He didn't see anything directly — no attacker, no creature, no
  obvious cause. His colleague was simply there one moment and gone the next,
  or he found signs of a struggle but no body. He can point the party to the
  specific area where it happened.
- **What he provides:** a location — the general area of the abductions, which
  is the party's starting point for finding the dump site and/or ritual site.
  He also provides an emotional anchor: the first taste of what the stakes
  feel like on a personal level.

### The Mortefactor Tracker
- **Race:** TBD (Deathless)
- **Faction:** The Most Ancient and Noble Order of Mortefactors
- **Role:** A faction agent sent to quietly track Šauhahten after his expulsion.
  He has been in or around Venna Weald for some time, but lost the trail. The
  party's evidence from the ritual site is exactly what he's been looking for.
- **Perspective:** The Mortefactors want Šauhahten stopped for their own reasons
  — he's using stolen research, giving their work a bad name, and possibly
  endangering something the faction cares about. The tracker is cooperative
  but has his own agenda; he may want to recover the research rather than
  simply destroy it, which could create friction with the party.
- **What he provides:** identification of the ritual techniques as
  Mortefactor-derived, Šauhahten's name and the circumstances of his
  expulsion, knowledge of what Prima Materia research requires (Fog proximity,
  ambient magical energy — which points to the Faerie site), and potentially
  the key to entering the laboratory (a Faerie resonance cipher that
  Šauhahten stole, which the Mortefactors have a partial copy of or know
  how to replicate).
- **Dramatic function:** an ally with strings attached. The party gains crucial
  information but may have to negotiate what happens to the research after
  the lab is destroyed. This introduces faction politics at a manageable
  scale.

### A Local Who Remembers Šauhahten
- **Race:** TBD (likely mortal)
- **Role:** A civilian — a shopkeeper, a supplier, someone who interacted with
  Šauhahten before he withdrew completely to the outskirts.
- **Perspective:** Not involved in the plot, just someone who remembers a
  remarkable customer. A Mirror is hard to forget in a frontier settlement.
- **What they provide:** personal details that humanize the villain before the
  confrontation. What he looked like, how he spoke, what he bought (unusual
  alchemical supplies, specific materials), which direction he traveled. These
  details fill in the picture and may help narrow down the laboratory's
  location (supply routes, frequency of visits, what he was buying in bulk).

## Pacing (~8 Sessions)

The adventure is designed to take approximately 8 sessions, which should
yield enough XP (~1500–2000 earned) to bring characters from their starting
1000 XP to roughly 2500–3000 cumulative — solidly through Tier 1 and ready
to begin purchasing Tier 2 advances.

- **Sessions 1–2: Phase 1** — Investigation in Keshto Village. Social
  interaction, skill checks, gathering leads. Ends when the party has a
  location to explore in the field.
- **Sessions 3–4: Phase 2** — Field exploration. The dump site and ritual
  site, in either order. Hazardous terrain, Fog exposure, investigation
  skills. First combat likely here (wildlife, Fog creatures, or a trap).
- **Session 5: Phase 3** — Return to Keshto Village with evidence. The
  Mortefactor connection, faction politics, putting the pieces together.
  Ends when the party knows who, what, and where.
- **Sessions 6–8: Phase 4** — The laboratory. Layered dungeon with combat,
  puzzles, and the climactic confrontation with Šauhahten. The bulk of
  combat XP comes from this phase.

### XP Budget

- **Mission XP:** 100 XP for the primary mission (destroying the lab),
  plus optionally 100 XP for a secondary objective (e.g. recovering the
  Mortefactor research intact, or confirming the patron's existence).
  Mission XP is deliberately modest — the bulk comes from combat and loot.
- **Combat XP:** From undead sentries, the alchemical monstrosity (if
  fought), Šauhahten himself, and any field encounters. Varies by party
  approach.
- **Loot XP:** 1 XP per 10 feathers of value recovered — salvaged
  alchemical equipment, Mortefactor research fragments, general dungeon
  loot. The laboratory should contain meaningful salvage.

### Wealth Budget

Feathers need to be plentiful enough to cover equipment maintenance costs
across intermissions. With T1 gear, a character might owe 100–200 feathers
per intermission (10% of total equipment base price), and the adventure
should have 2–3 intermissions. Between the Black Guard contract payout,
loot from the field sites, and laboratory salvage, each character should
come out ahead — but not so far ahead that T2 gear is trivially affordable
by the end. The adventure should feel like working for a living, not a
windfall.

## Rewards and Outcomes

- **Black Guard contract payout:** The party's primary payment for
  completing the mission. Amount should be appropriate for T1 (somewhere
  in the 500–1500 feather range depending on perceived difficulty).
- **Laboratory salvage:** Alchemical equipment, rare reagents, fragments
  of Mortefactor research. What the party can keep depends on the
  negotiation with the Mortefactor tracker — he wants the research back.
- **Faction reputation:** Completing the contract builds standing with
  the Never-Still. How the party handles the tracker determines their
  relationship with the Mortefactors (cooperative, antagonistic, or
  indebted).
- **Open threads for future adventures:**
  - Šauhahten will reincorporate and may seek revenge or resume his work
    elsewhere.
  - The patron in the Fog is still out there, its nature undefined —
    ready for the GM to develop.
  - The Mortefactors may come calling if the party owes them a favor, or
    if the recovered research reveals something dangerous.

## Failure States

The adventure should be resilient to partial failure — the party can
stumble at individual steps without the whole thing collapsing. The GM
guide should include these fallbacks:

- **Can't find both field sites:** The accelerating disappearances produce
  more evidence over time. The surviving peacekeeper or Merzaghaal can
  provide a nudge toward the missing location if the party is stuck.
- **Party alerts Šauhahten early:** If the party is loud or slow through
  the laboratory's outer layers, Šauhahten begins destroying his most
  sensitive research. The party arrives to a partially emptied lab —
  reduced loot and incomplete answers, but the mission is still
  completable. He cannot move the heavy equipment.
- **Party agrees with Šauhahten:** He asks them to bring him more
  subjects. This is a moral trap — the adventure should flag this moment
  for the GM and make it viscerally clear what the process does to souls
  (the souls are aware, they suffer). If the party still agrees, the
  Mortefactor tracker or Merzaghaal intervenes, and the party has made
  enemies.
- **Can't solve the cipher/entrance:** The Mortefactor tracker can provide
  a partial solution as a fallback, at the cost of the party owing the
  Mortefactors a faction favor — a concrete hook for future adventures.
- **TPK in the laboratory:** The party is Deathless — they reincorporate
  at their soul anchors after a few days. When they return, Šauhahten has
  relocated, taking what he could carry. The mission is a failure in the
  immediate sense, but the laboratory is disrupted (he lost the heavy
  equipment and the site itself), and the party has all the information
  they need to track him again. This sets up a sequel rather than a retry.
